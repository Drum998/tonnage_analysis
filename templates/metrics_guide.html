Metrics page guide

The Metrics page is a quick way to understand activity (how many lots), volume (how many kg), value (GBP), and price behavior (level, spread, and volatility) for a single species over a date range. Most charts are smoothed with a 21-day moving average to reduce day-to-day noise.

Important
  • All calculations are based on the raw lots in the selected date range after species normalization.
  • Units: tonnage is shown as kg and value is in GBP (GBP symbol shown as "GBP" in this guide).
  • Smoothing can hide short spikes; if you need exact daily values, treat the charts as trend indicators.

Filters and presets

  • Species: Select the species you want to analyze (normalized names are used internally).
  • Date range: The metrics include only lots whose market date falls between start_date and end_date (inclusive).
  • Preset buttons: Quick date windows (Last Week, Last Month, etc.). These only set the date inputs; click "Load Metrics" to refresh charts.

Summary cards (top row)

Total Lots

Count of lots for the selected species and date range. Use it as an activity indicator: rising lot counts can mean more consistent landings or more fragmented lotting.

Total Tonnage (kg)

Sum of lot weights in kg for the selected period. Use it as a volume indicator. If tonnage rises but lots do not, average lot size may be increasing.

Total Value

Sum of Price/KG * Weight across all included lots. Use it to understand economic importance of the period, and to compare weeks/months (with care for seasonality).

Avg Price/KG

Overall average of lot Price/KG across the period (simple average by lot, not weighted by kg). Use it as a quick price level indicator, but pair it with median and volatility for context.

WoW Value Change

Week-over-week percentage change in value based on the last 7 days vs the prior 7 days inside your selected range. It appears only when there is enough data (at least 14 days in the selection, and prior-week value is not zero).

MoM Value Change

Month-over-month percentage change in value based on the last 30 days vs the prior 30 days inside your selected range. It appears only when there is enough data (at least 60 days in the selection, and prior-month value is not zero).

Price-Tonnage Correlation

Correlation between daily average price/kg and daily total tonnage over the selected range. A negative value can suggest higher volumes are associated with lower prices (and vice versa), but correlation is not causation.

Charts

Lot Count and Total Value Over Time

  • Lot Count: 21-day moving average of daily lot counts.
  • Total Value (GBP): 21-day moving average of daily total value (sum of Price/KG * Weight).

Use this chart to separate "more activity" from "higher value". For example, value can rise because prices rise, tonnage rises, or both (even if lot count is flat).

Price Distribution (Min, Max, Median, Avg) Over Time

  • Min / Max Price/KG: Daily minimum and maximum lot price/kg (then smoothed).
  • Median Price/KG: Daily median lot price/kg (then smoothed). Often more robust to outliers than the average.
  • Avg Price/KG: Daily average lot price/kg (then smoothed).

Use this chart to understand whether changes in "typical" price (median) are happening, or whether only extremes are moving (min/max widening). If median is flat but max is rising, premium lots may be driving headlines.

Average Lot Size Over Time

21-day moving average of daily average lot size in kg (daily total kg divided by daily lot count). Use it to detect shifts in market structure: larger lots can change price behavior and volatility.

Price Volatility (Std Dev, Coefficient of Variation)

  • Price Std Dev (GBP/kg): Standard deviation of lot price/kg within each day (then smoothed).
  • Coefficient of Variation (%): \((StdDev / AvgPrice) * 100\) per day (then smoothed).

Use volatility to judge confidence in "the price". High volatility means the market is paying very different prices for lots on the same day (quality, size, gear, buyer mix, and reporting can all contribute).

Gear breakdown (when available)

If gear data exists for the selected species and period, the page shows two additional charts:

  • Gear Breakdown by Tonnage: doughnut chart showing kg by gear, plus percent share of tonnage.
  • Gear Breakdown by Value: horizontal bars showing total GBP value by gear, plus percent share of total value.

Use these to answer questions like "Is most of the volume/value coming from a single gear type?" and to spot mix shifts that can explain changes in price and volatility.

Practical usage patterns

  • Trend check: Start with Lot Count + Total Value to see whether change is driven by activity or economics.
  • Price sanity: Compare median vs average. If they diverge, outliers may be influencing the average.
  • Stability: Use CV (%) to compare volatility across species or periods (it normalizes by price level).
  • Mix effects: If price moves, check gear charts and average lot size to see if composition changed.
